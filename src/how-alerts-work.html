How local emergency alerts work

Emergency alerts work like a radio broadcast.

During an emergency, mobile phone masts in the affected area will broadcast an alert. Every compatible mobile phone or tablet in range of a mast will get the alert.

The emergency services do not need your phone number to send you an alert.

You get alerts based on where you are, not who you are or where you live.

Emergency alerts are free and you do not need to sign up for them or download an app.

No one collects or shares data about you, your phone or your location when you receive an alert.

Your phone can still receive alerts if you’re on a call.

You will not get alerts if your phone or tablet is turned off or in aeroplane mode.

You can opt out of local emergency alerts, but we recommend that you keep them switched on for your own safety.

Phone handsets and devices

Emergency alerts work on most phones and tablets made after 2015. Always make sure your device has all the latest software updates.

If your device was made before 2015, search your settings for ‘emergency alerts’ to check they’re turned on.

Mobile phone networks

Emergency alerts work on all UK 4G and 5G phone networks.

Phones and tablets connected to a 2G or 3G network will not receive emergency alerts.

Emergency alerts do not cause, and are not affected by, busy phone networks.
